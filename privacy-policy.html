Privacy Policy: EDI Data Handling & Security

To pass the Chrome Web Store's "Enhanced Protection" review, this policy outlines our transparent practices regarding how raw EDI strings—which may contain sensitive commercial data—are processed and secured.

1. Data Collection & Usage

We collect raw X12 EDI transmissions (specifically segments such as ISA, GS, and transaction sets like 350, 309, and 315) only when the user explicitly initiates a "Validation" or "Audit" request.

  • Purpose: Data is used exclusively to perform structural compliance checks against CBP CATAIR specifications.
  • Limitation: We do not scrape or collect data in the background. Data collection is strictly user-triggered.

2. Data Retention (The "Ephemeral" Standard)

To ensure the security of your shipping and manifest data, we adhere to strict retention limits:

  • Validation Data: Raw EDI strings are processed in memory and are not permanently stored on our servers. Once the validation report is generated, the raw input is purged from the session.
  • Metadata: We may store non-sensitive metadata (e.g., "350 Ocean Manifest validated successfully") to provide users with a history of their activity and to manage credit balances.

3. Encryption & Transit

All data transmitted between the extension and our validation engine is encrypted using TLS 1.2/1.3 (SSL). We utilize AES-256 encryption for any sensitive configuration data stored within the user's chrome.storage.sync area.

4. Third-Party Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent user EDI data to third parties. Data is never shared with logistics competitors, marketing firms, or data brokers.
